"Sailing to Byzantium"

Theme

- Yeats's recreation of Byzantium
  => Desire for the unity of being and culture deficient in his real world.
- Byzantium as a religious, civilized, and aristocratic society
- Desire for the immortality of his poetry.
  =>Representation of "universal" values of the liberal subjectivity
-Form: Octava rima - Renaissance form - connoting Yeats's preference for ancient literature, culture, and civilization.

Two Types of Interpretation

1. 나이든 사람이 젊은 사람을 보는 시각. '나는 예술품이 될 거야.'
2. Yeats는 끝까지 육체적인 욕망을 버리지 않았다.
